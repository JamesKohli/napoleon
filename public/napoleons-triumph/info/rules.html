Napoleon's Triumph

Rules Summary

Overview

Two players command the French and Allied armies at the Battle of Austerlitz (December 2, 1805). Victory is achieved by breaking enemy morale or controlling objectives.

Turn Sequence

  1. Allied player turn
  2. French player turn
  3. Advance round marker

Commands

CommandTypeEffect
Corps MoveCorpsMove commander and all units in corps together
Detach MoveCorpsDetach units from corps and move them (no road)
AttachCorpsAttach a unit in same position to corps
Unit MoveIndependentMove single detached unit (can use road)

Command Limits:

  • Allied: 5 corps commands, 3 independent commands per turn
  • French: Unlimited corps commands, 4 independent commands per turn

Movement

  • From Reserve: To adjacent reserve OR to block an approach in same locale
  • From Approach: To reserve in same locale OR to reserve across the approach
  • Road Movement: Main road = 3 locales, Local road = 2 locales

Combat Procedure (11 Steps)

  1. Attack Threat: Attacker names the approach
  2. Retreat Option: Defender may retreat or name defending pieces
  3. Feint Option: Attacker may declare a feint
  4. Defense Declaration: Defender names leading units (0-2)
  5. Attack Declaration: Attacker names command, width, and leading units
  6. Initial Result: Compare strengths with modifiers
  7. Counter-Attack: Defender may counter-attack
  8. Final Result: Apply counter-attack to determine winner
  9. Attacker Losses: Calculate and apply attacker casualties
  10. Defender Losses: Calculate and apply defender casualties
  11. Completion: Retreat or advance as appropriate

Victory

  • Decisive Victory: Reduce enemy morale to 0
  • Marginal Victory: Control objectives at game end

Starting Morale

  • French: 23
  • Allied: 27
